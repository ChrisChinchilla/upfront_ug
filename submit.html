About the talks at up.front

  • Audience: Up.front is a meetup focused on web design and frontend development topics. Our audience is a diverse mix of designers, frontend developers, concept, UX and communications people.
  • Topics: The talks tend to be relatively light on code and cover topics such as development strategies, design language, managing a front end team, user testing, publishing a magazine, the web in other cultures and languages, colour theory, surviving as a freelancer, CSS preprocessors, storytelling in products, web performance and many more. Just scroll through the list of previous talks and you’ll get a good sense of what we’re about.
  • Experience level: We’re open to anyone, from bloody beginners to conference veterans. Here are some good reasons to start speaking at meetups.
  • Language: All talks are in English.
  • Talk Duration: Talks should be no longer than 20 minutes. In addition, there will be 3-5 minutes time for Q&A. Please time your presentation in advance, so we don’t have to cut you off.
  • Slides and Equipment: There’s a projector (1280x800 native resolution) with HDMI, VGA and Mini DisplayPort connectors, and the choice between a wireless microphone headset and a wired hand mic. We can also get audio from your machine into the speakers via AirPlay or 3.5mm audio jack, if needed.

If you’re unsure about any of these fields, check out the front page for examples, or ask us if anything is unclear. Alright then, let’s get started!
