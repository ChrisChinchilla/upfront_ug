Speakers Guide



Dear speaker,

We’re happy you’d like to give a talk at up.front.ug! Our meetup is focused on web design and frontend development topics. Every month we meet at co.up, a co-working space in Kreuzberg, where we regularly attract a crowd of 70 people and more, most of them frontend engineers and webdesigners. Our events are free and open for everyone, questions and discussions are encouraged.

Your Talk and Required Information

Talks should be no longer than 20 minutes. We’ll provide a countdown so you can check your timing during the talk. We do not take questions from the audience – there’ll be time for discussion at the end of the meetup.

Please provide us with the following information so we can announce your talk , also make sure that your abstract and bio are written as third person statements (e.g. "In this talk Laura will talk about…" instead of "In this talk I will talk about…"):

  • Your Name: Title of your talk

    Short abstract (3-5 sentences) of your talk, e.g.: What is your main question? What is your hypothesis? What will people learn? If it is a technical talk, what is the level (beginner, advanced, expert) and what knowledge is needed to be able to follow along (e.g. HTML, CSS)?

    A sentence about yourself, what you are working on or whatever you want to let people know about yourself.

    • @your_twitter_handle
    • Your website
    • Other relevant links
    For examples, browse our archive of past events.

Location and Schedule

Our meetups take place at co.up Coworking Space, Adalbertstraße 7, 10999 Berlin.

We recommend you show up at 7pm, and find one of our organizers. We’ll help you to set up your notebook, and make sure you have a cool drink. Talks start around 7:30pm. If you prefer to speak at the end or beginning of the line-up, or have to leave early / arrive later, please let us know in advance.

Technical Equipment

The kind people at co.up provide us with an Epson EX7200 Projector (Native Resolution: 1280x800 (WXGA)) that projects onto a clear white wall.

You can project your screen either via AirPlay (Mac OS X >= 10.8) or the HDMI/VGA ports of the projector. Please bring your own adapter (HDMI is preferred), we usually have spare adapters at the venue but we can’t promise anything.

You can use the speakers either via AirPlay or a standard audio cable. The venue is equipped with an audio system and we have wireless headset microphones and hand microphones available.

Commercial Announcements in Talks

We are very grateful for you to speak at up.front! So if you want to let the audience know about your cool Open Source project, an event you are organizing, or that your company is hiring, you are very welcome to mention that while you’re on stage. Just please keep commercial shout-outs short and excite the crowd with the content of your talk.

Lightning Talks

For small announcements or very quick topics we can also give you a slot for a lightning talk. They are usually held before or after a break. Please make sure you stay within the following requirements:

  • Lightning talks shouldn't be longer than 2-3 minutes. After 5 minutes we will ask you to stop immediately but you should not take the chance.
  • You should not have more than one slide.
  • Our requirements for commercial announcements also apply for lightning talks.

If you would like to have a lightning talk slot please let us know at least a day in advance.

Questions?

Please contact us any time if you have questions – we’re here to help! Up.front welcomes speakers of any level of experience. Never held a talk before or need assistance in improving your skills? Let us know, and we’ll review your slides and notes or give feedback and tips. Here’s an excellent resource about getting into public speaking: weareallaweso.me

We’re looking forward to have you! Cheers,
Alex, Katha, Kristina and Max
